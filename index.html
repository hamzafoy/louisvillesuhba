About Us

بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم الحمد لله حق حمده، والصلاة و السلام على محمد رسوله و عبده، و على آله و أصحابه من بعده. أما بعد

In the name of Allah, Most Gracious & Most Merciful - All Praises are due to Allah, as much as His right to be praised. And peace and blessings be upon Muhammad, His Messenger and slave, and upon his Family and his Companions after him.


The Louisville Suhba is a group of young men, around the ages of 18 & older, who intentionally wish to improve themselves wholly. In the Islamic tradition, there is no separation of "faith" from the "secular" and this means to our young men that we should strive to attain excellence in our ritual worship, our personal character, in our personal finances, in our career or trades, in our participation in the community, and in our chivalry in how we look out for others.

Our group has an ameer, but work together to ensure that our activities are beneficial and that all of our members are engaged and participating. Currently, our group has decided to attend an announced gathering every week throughout the month. Our activities vary between the social & recreational (such as soccer, basketball, or going out to a venue), gathering to discuss Islamically themed topics or learning a skill or personally beneficial knowledge, and gathering with Imam Wasif Iqbal or any other local scholar to understand more complex topics in our religion.

Suhba Projects

وَتَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْبِرِّ وَالتَّقْوَىٰ ۖ وَلَا تَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْإِثْمِ وَالْعُدْوَانِ

(O you who believe) . . . help one another in piety and goodness and do not help one another in sin and aggression

Our first project as Louisville Suhba members is creating a raised bed to add onto the new landscaping at Guiding Light Islamic Center. This raised bed will contain herbs and/or vegetables for the community to pick and eat from. Our declared intention for this project is to serve as a source of sadaqa from our brothers to the general Muslim community.

Narrated by Anas ibn Malik: Allah's Messenger (ﷺ) said, "There is none amongst the Muslims who plants a tree or sows seeds, and then a bird, or a person or an animal eats from it, but is regarded as a charitable gift for him."
